How Long Does
Local SEO Actually
Take to Work?

A month-by-month breakdown of what to expect - and what a good agency should be showing you along the way.

20 January 2026  ·  Local SEO · 7 min read

If you’ve been burned by an agency before - paid for months, saw nothing change, couldn’t get a straight answer - this is written for you. The honest truth about local SEO timelines is that it takes longer than most agencies admit upfront, but also delivers results earlier than most clients expect in specific areas. The problem is that no one draws the line clearly enough between what changes fast, what takes months, and what to watch for if nothing’s happening.

This is the realistic timeline Haylo uses with every new Sunshine Coast client. No vague promises about “results in 90 days” - just what actually happens, week by week and month by month, and what you should be able to measure at each stage.

What Happens in the First Two Weeks of Local SEO?

Weeks one and two are audit and fix weeks. A good agency goes through your Google Business Profile and identifies every incomplete or incorrect field: wrong categories, missing services, inconsistent contact details, absent photos, no description, incorrect hours. These are fixed immediately. The fixes don’t produce ranking jumps overnight but they remove obstacles that were actively suppressing your visibility. In week two, a review collection process should be up and running - whether that’s a simple text message template, an automated follow-up, or a QR code card you give to every customer. You should also have a baseline: where do you currently rank for your main search terms? This number needs to be documented so you can measure movement.

What Can You Expect in the First Month?

Month one is when the first visible changes appear - but they are usually not ranking changes. You will see your first new reviews coming in if your collection process is working. You should see your profile’s photo count increasing. If your profile had significant issues (wrong category, missing services), some search visibility improvements can appear as Google re-indexes your profile. Most Sunshine Coast businesses in less competitive suburbs start to see ranking movement within four to six weeks. In more competitive areas like Maroochydore or Noosa, movement in month one is modest. This is normal. The groundwork laid in weeks one and two is what the next three months build on.

When Do Rankings Start to Move Noticeably?

Months two and three are when ranking movement becomes visible and measurable. If your review count has been growing, you’re posting regular photos, and your profile is complete, Google starts treating you as an active and established business. Most Haylo clients see movement into the top five to eight positions during this window. You may also start appearing in searches for suburb-specific terms you weren’t ranking for at all before. This is also when calls typically start increasing - not dramatically, but noticeably. Document this. When your phone rings from Google Maps traffic, note it. This is data that proves the direction of movement.

When Should You Expect Consistent Top 3 Results?

Months three to six is when the top three becomes realistic for most Sunshine Coast markets. This depends heavily on competition. A carpet cleaner in Nambour will get there faster than a plumber in Maroochydore. What matters is sustained activity: consistent reviews coming in, active profile, photos updated regularly. By month six, businesses that have followed this process are typically holding top three positions for their main search terms and appearing for a broader range of suburb and service-specific searches. Month six is also when the compounding effect of local SEO becomes obvious - a position you worked six months to achieve tends to stay. It doesn’t disappear the moment you reduce spending, unlike paid ads.

Quick Win: On day one with any new agency, ask them to document your current baseline rankings for five specific search terms relevant to your business and suburb. If they can’t show you that number changing over three months, something is wrong.

If you’ve been paying an agency for more than three months and have seen no movement in reviews, no increase in calls from Google, and no ranking changes you can actually measure - that’s a problem. Good local SEO produces measurable changes within four to eight weeks even if the final ranking goals take longer. You should always be able to see the direction of travel.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to rank on Google Maps for a local business on the Sunshine Coast?

Most Sunshine Coast service businesses see measurable ranking movement within 4 to 8 weeks of starting a proper local SEO program. Getting into the top three in less competitive suburbs can happen within 2 to 3 months. More competitive areas like Maroochydore or Noosa typically take 3 to 6 months of consistent work. The key variables are how complete your current profile is, how many reviews you have compared to competitors, and how consistently you add new content.

What should I be able to measure in the first 30 days of local SEO?

In the first 30 days you should be able to measure: your baseline rankings documented at the start, new reviews added during the period, new photos added to your profile, and any changes to profile completeness. You may not see significant ranking movement in 30 days, but you should see clear evidence that the groundwork is being done and that the review collection system is producing results.

Is local SEO worth it for a small business on the Sunshine Coast?

Yes - for most service businesses on the Sunshine Coast, local SEO is the highest-ROI marketing activity available. Unlike paid advertising, the results compound over time and don’t disappear when you stop paying. A business that reaches the top three on Google Maps for its primary search terms typically generates a consistent stream of inbound calls without ongoing ad spend. The upfront investment in getting there is the cost; the ongoing maintenance is comparatively low.

Why did my previous agency show no results after 6 months?

Common reasons include: the agency was doing work on a website rather than your Google Business Profile, no active review collection system was in place, the baseline was never documented so there was nothing to measure against, or the reporting was vague rather than tied to specific ranking positions. Local SEO for service businesses in 2026 is primarily about your Google Business Profile, review volume, and consistent activity - not website changes. If the agency wasn’t focused on those three things, results are unlikely regardless of the timeframe.
